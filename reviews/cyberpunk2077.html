The Last of Us Part II Remastered

PC (Steam) | AMD Ryzen 9 3900X 12-Core | NVIDIA Geforce RTX 4070 Super

10/10

Summary

The Last of Us Part II Remastered is an emotionally intense and beautifully crafted action-adventure game that builds on the foundation laid by its predecessor. Set in a post-apocalyptic world, you play as Ellie, a young woman in seek of vengeance. The narrative is driven by strong characters, tough moral decisions, and a world that feels both hauntingly real and deeply tragic. The game is built on a foundation of solid gameplay, with brutal and strategic combat, exploration, and a sense of survival that keeps you engaged from start to finish.

The world design is truly outstanding, with lush environments, intricate details, and a rich atmosphere that pulls you in. The performances of the voice actors, especially Ellie and other key characters, are exceptional, making you feel each emotional beat of the story. Whether you're invested in the narrative or purely here for the gameplay, The Last of Us Part II Remastered is a game that makes an impact. It's a dark, beautiful journey with moments of hope, despair, and everything in between—worth experiencing for anyone who appreciates well-crafted, story-driven games.

Audio

  • ☑ Exceptional – Music and sound design are unforgettable
  • ☐ Great – Well done, really enhances the game
  • ☐ Good – Works fine, nothing too remarkable
  • ☐ Average – Not bad, but lacks impact
  • ☐ Poor – Distracting or unpolished sound
  • ☐ Terrible – Unbearable, drags the experience down

An audiophiles dream. To put this into perspective, the audio design in this game is so beyond good, that a blind person can beat this game in it's entirety. While that certainly says a ton about the accessibility options available in this game (which are seemingly infinite) it also says that Naughty Dog put as much effort into the audio as they did every other aspect of the game. The dialogue sounds like its coming from the characters where they are in relation to the player, rather than sounding flat. Sound mastering changes with the environment; for example, firing a gun in a silo causes a deafening bang, causing the characters to react. The soundtrack is beautiful and is a perfect match for the tone of the games story. Highly immersive audio for a highly immersive game.

  • ☐ Very Easy – Anyone can pick it up and play

While there is a difficulty selection in this game, even playing on the normal difficulty mode poses some unexpected challenges that will require you to assess the situation strategically and plan your approach to different situations. The enemy AI is very smart and will strategize their approach to you just as well. Too easy for you? Crank it up to Grounded difficulty, where resources are scarce, enemy intelligence is cranked up to 11, companions provide little to no assistance in combat, you have no HUD or Listen Mode, and checkpoints are fewer and farther between. Still too easy? Enable Permadeath for full Masochist mode.

I hold the story of The Last of Us Part II in the highest regard—not just as a game, but as one of the most powerful narratives I've ever experienced in any medium. It's hauntingly beautiful, unflinchingly tragic, and emotionally devastating. The game is a masterclass in character study, using the cycle of vengeance and justice to force the player into uncomfortable moral territory. What sets it apart is how it doesn't just ask you to witness its themes—it makes you live them through its characters. It's a story that breaks you down, piece by piece, and yet somehow finds beauty in the ruin. This story will stick with me for the rest of my life.

With a main story averaging around 24 hours, The Last of Us Part II Remastered delivers a full, impactful experience without overstaying its welcome. For those craving more, the new “No Return” mode offers extended replayability. This roguelike survival mode lets you choose a character and progress through randomized combat scenarios, upgrading your skills and weapons along the way, culminating in a boss fight at the end of each run.

At the time of writing this, The Last of Us Part II Remastered does have a few bugs—much like the PC port of Part I. In No Return mode, some players have experienced graphical issues, such as parts of the map disappearing. The frequent loading in this mode can also be a drawback, with load times sometimes taking 30 seconds to a full minute between scenarios. Thankfully, these times tend to improve with repeated play, but they may still be frustrating for some.
